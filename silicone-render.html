What is external wall insulation?

External wall insulation (EWI) fixes insulation boards to external walls, then encases them in a reinforced base coat and through-coloured render topcoat. It is ideal for solid-wall homes that cannot take cavity insulation.

In entity terms: EWI is-a external thermal insulation system; its layers are insulation board, base coat with mesh, and render topcoat; its benefit is reduced heat loss; its standard reference is Building Regulations Approved Document L. It is closely related to silicone render, which is the usual finish coat.

How much does external wall insulation cost per m²?

EWI with a render finish costs from £100/m² installed, varying with insulation thickness and wall area. A detached home is a significant project priced after a detailed survey.

What are the benefits of external wall insulation?

EWI reduces heat loss, lowers energy bills and adds a new rendered facade in one job, with no internal disruption and no lost room space.

  • Warmer home, lower bills — solid walls lose heat fast; EWI keeps it in.
  • No internal mess — all work is external.
  • New facade — insulation and render in a single project.
  • Reduces condensation — warmer internal wall surfaces.

Does external wall insulation need planning permission?

EWI is often permitted development, but because it changes wall thickness and appearance, listed buildings and conservation areas may need consent. We check and handle any application.
