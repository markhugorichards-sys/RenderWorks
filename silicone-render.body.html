What is silicone render?

Silicone render is a polymer-modified, thin-coat decorative topcoat applied over a reinforced base coat on external walls. The binder is silicone resin, which makes the cured surface hydrophobic (water-repellent) and vapour-permeable (breathable). It is supplied through-coloured, so pigment runs through the full topcoat thickness and the wall never needs repainting.

In entity terms: the product is-a thin-coat render; its binder is silicone resin; its typical thickness is 1.5–5 mm; its application method is hand-float or spray; its substrate is a cement or lightweight base coat reinforced with alkali-resistant fibreglass mesh. Adjacent systems include monocouche render, acrylic render, mineral render and External Wall Insulation (EWI), of which silicone is the standard finishing coat.

How much does silicone render cost per m² in Surrey?

Silicone render costs £60–£120/m² installed in Guildford and Surrey. The figure includes scaffold, beads, fibreglass mesh, base coat, primer and through-coloured topcoat. Removal of failed render or pebbledash adds approximately 15–20% to the labour element.

Is silicone render more expensive than monocouche?

Yes. Silicone render costs £60–£120/m² versus £45–£90/m² for monocouche render. The price premium buys the self-cleaning hydrophobic topcoat and greater crack resistance from the flexible silicone binder. For a like-for-like comparison across all systems, see our render cost guide.

What are the properties of silicone render?

Silicone render is hydrophobic, vapour-permeable, crack-resistant and self-cleaning. These four attributes are the reason it is specified over acrylic and sand-cement finishes in the UK climate.

  • Hydrophobic — water beads and runs off (the lotus effect), reducing water ingress and frost damage.
  • Vapour-permeable — moisture vapour escapes the wall, lowering interstitial condensation and damp risk on solid walls.
  • Self-cleaning — rainwater lifts surface dirt and biological growth, suppressing algae and black spot.
  • Crack-resistant — the flexible silicone binder accommodates thermal movement better than rigid cementitious renders.
  • Through-coloured — colour is integral to the topcoat; no repainting across the service life.
  • UV-stable — pigment and binder resist fade under ultraviolet exposure.

Does silicone render prevent damp?

Silicone render reduces penetrating damp by repelling rainwater and reduces condensation-related damp by remaining breathable. It does not cure rising damp or defective DPC (damp-proof course) failures, which are structural and must be remediated before rendering. We test for moisture at survey.

What is the best silicone render system?

The most-specified UK silicone render systems are K-Rend, Weber (Saint-Gobain), Parex and Krend. All are BBA-certified silicone or silicone-silicate systems applied as a full kit: base coat, alkali-resistant mesh, primer and topcoat.

System selection is governed by substrate, exposure zone (BS 8104 driving-rain index), and whether External Wall Insulation is included. Warranty validity depends on certified-applicator installation to the manufacturer specification — mixed components or non-approved fixings void cover. See our K-Rend & Weber application page.

How is silicone render applied?

Silicone render is applied in five stages over 5–10 working days for a typical semi-detached house. The substrate is prepared, a base coat embeds the mesh, beads define the edges, a primer is applied, and the through-coloured topcoat is floated or sprayed to the chosen grain.

  1. Preparation — substrate cleaned; failed render hacked off; substrate stabilised.
  2. Beading — stainless or PVC stop beads, bell-cast beads and corner beads set to true the lines.
  3. Base coat + mesh — cementitious base coat applied; alkali-resistant fibreglass mesh embedded for crack control.
  4. Primer — tinted primer keys the topcoat and regulates suction.
  5. Topcoat — silicone topcoat applied at 1.0–1.5 mm grain and rubbed up to a uniform texture.

Application requires air and substrate temperatures of 5–30 °C and dry conditions during cure. Surrey scheduling therefore avoids frost windows and prolonged rain.

Do you need planning permission for silicone render?

No, in most cases. Re-rendering is normally permitted development in England. Permission is required for listed buildings, properties in a conservation area, and where an Article 4 direction applies — several exist around Guildford and Godalming. We confirm planning status at survey and submit any application required.

How long does silicone render last?

Silicone render lasts 25–40 years with minimal maintenance. Maintenance is limited to an occasional low-pressure rinse; the hydrophobic surface suppresses the algae and staining that age traditional finishes.
